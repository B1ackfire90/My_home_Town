Brahmanbaria District, Bangladesh

Introduction

Brahmanbaria is a district in eastern Bangladesh, part of the Chittagong Division. Known for its cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and scenic rivers, it plays a significant role in the country's history and economy.

Key Facts

  • Established: 1984
  • Area: 1,927.11 kmÂ²
  • Population: ~3.2 million
  • Major Rivers: Titas, Meghna, Buri
  • Upazilas: 9 (including Brahmanbaria Sadar, Ashuganj, Nasirnagar)

Historical & Cultural Significance

Brahmanbaria is the birthplace of famous poets like Al Mahmud and Syed Shamsul Haq. The district also has ties to the Bhawal Sannyasi Movement against British rule. The historic Uttara Ganabhaban palace is located here.

Tourist Attractions

  • Titas River & Gas Field: Scenic beauty and historical importance.
  • Haripur Barabari: A 19th-century zamindar palace.
  • Nasirnagar Indigenous Villages: Home to Tripura and Manipuri communities.
  • Bhairab-Rasulpur Railway Bridge: Iconic bridge over the Meghna River.
